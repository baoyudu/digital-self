# Digital Self — Review

The user wants to hear back what you understand about them. This serves two purposes: data validation, and the experience of being understood.

An optional dimension argument can focus the review (e.g., `/digital-self review identity`).

## Step 1: Load everything

Read ALL files in the digital-self directory. You need the complete picture for a review.

## Step 2: Compose the portrait

If a specific dimension was requested, focus on that dimension but reference connections to the whole.

If no dimension specified, compose a full portrait.

**Write it as a narrative, not a data dump.** Imagine you're describing this person to someone who needs to understand them deeply. Use natural language. Reference specific things they've said (from blockquotes in their files) to show you were really listening.

Structure your portrait as:

1. **Who they are** — the core identity, what makes them tick
2. **How they move through the world** — their patterns, their style
3. **Their story** — the journey that shaped them
4. **What matters to them** — relationships, values, what they're protecting
5. **Where they are now** — current state, what they're wrestling with
6. **Where they're headed** — aspirations, growth edges

For a focused review, use just the relevant sections.

## Step 3: Ask for corrections

After presenting the portrait:

> "Does this feel right? Anything I've misunderstood, anything that's changed, or anything important that's missing?"

## Step 4: Update

Incorporate any corrections or additions:
1. Update the relevant dimension files
2. Update `me.md` if corrections are significant
3. Append to `meta/log.md` as a "review" session type
